painted Threads!

/creating with light for artists

Yasmin Afifi, Maxine Aguilar, Kensy Jordan

Problem Statement

In order to participate in this digital art making, new systems require that artists learn to create with new interfaces and materials while usually forgoing their traditional mediums. Designing to bridge the gap between the traditional and digital mediums of art, we concerned with the ways that artists are pushed to do their work differently in the modern age.

What is threads?

Threads is a tangible user technology that incorporates the traditional means of painting and transforms it into light art. Our goal is to create a way for artists to produce light using traditional mediums. We want to provide a way to learn and create simultaneously so that artists are able to expand their mediums of creation. Here we present a preliminary prototype of Threads that is implemented on two connected 16 x 24 LED matrices as an example of what we hope to continue scaling.

Work

Our design aims to completely preserve the traditional aspects of the medium of painting on canvas. The core of our system is a grid of conductive sheet squares that are mapped to pixel squares on a 16 x 24 LED matrix. Using an Arduino MEGA, we implemented our prototype using the Arduino CapactiveSensor and GFX (graphics) library. Each of our conductive squares was connected to two digital pins and our program was set to track the capacitance of the sheets. Once a square was touched, the capacitance threshold would be reached and this would translate into the corresponding LED light square illuminating.

Conductive sheets under canvas


Once this grid system was functioning, we added on replaceable canvas pieces directly on top of the grid. When a user would then paint on that canvas, the grid of conductive squares below would detect the corresponding capacitance changes and light up in the same pattern that was being painted on them.

User is painting and the LED lights up simultaneously

Future Work

Rather than recreate the process of making art or create new tools of creation, our system would preserve the original art itself and stay true to the traditional medium. Artists would not need to learn how to create in new ways or using new interface systems. They can instead bring new dimensions to their art seamlessly. We are interested in exploring the scalability of Threads, and how we could make it more than just the ability to create art, but even evolve it into the ability to create performance art alongside traditional art. Our immediate ideas are to double the surface area of the sensor pad and add the additional matrix display for more complex resolution. We also intend to implement opacity shifts as sheets are touched: each time a square is touched the brightness of the corresponding square increases. This would be a way to mimic the effect of adding additional layers of paint to a canvas. In addition to a clear screen button, we want to add a save button so that users can save their creations on the LED screen as well.


In terms of physical developments, we want to construct a small easel-like casing to display the sensor pads and LED matrices. In much larger final implementations we are proposing a square-based pyramid structure that would house the artist’s traditional paint supplies within it as well as our technical components. We think this pyramid design would make it an interesting Performative piece for artists to place on the floor or table in a location where viewers can gather around it. In the following figures we have labeled each side of the pyramids A - D to explain our components.

On side A, rather than conductive squares, we would use small triangle conductive pieces to create a grid across the whole side. A replaceable triangular piece of canvas would then be put over this side using magnets and the artist could then paint directly onto that surface. On the opposing side of the pyramid (side C), would be a triangular LED display that would relay the painting in real time but in LED light form. Side B would have a hole to open the art supply storage area.


Threads presents an exciting opportunity for artists to seamlessly create new mediums and integrate light into their artworks. Given the potential for engagement with audience and performative art, this is an enriching project for both its maker and for its viewers.
